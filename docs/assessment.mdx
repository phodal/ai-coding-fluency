# Fluency Assessment Tool

import Assessment from '@site/src/components/FluencyModel/Assessment';

The AI Coding Fluency Assessment Tool helps you map out your current and aspired level of fluency for each dimension and identify the sensible-default set of investments required to achieve your goals.

Inspired by [ThoughtWorks Digital Fluency Model](https://www.thoughtworks.com/digital-fluency/digital-fluency-model), this tool provides:

- **Current State Assessment** - Identify where your team is today
- **Target Setting** - Define where you want to be
- **Investment Roadmap** - Get specific recommendations for each phase
- **Progress Tracking** - Your selections are automatically saved

<Assessment />

## How It Works

### Step 1: Select a Dimension
Click on any of the five building blocks to begin your assessment. Each dimension represents a critical aspect of AI-assisted development.

### Step 2: Assess Current Level
For each dimension, select the level that best describes your team's current state. Be honest - this is about understanding where you are, not where you want to be.

### Step 3: Set Target Level
Choose your aspiration level. This should be ambitious but achievable within your planning horizon (typically 6-18 months).

### Step 4: Review Investments
The tool will show you the recommended investments needed to progress from your current level to your target.

### Step 5: Generate Your Pattern
Once all dimensions are assessed, view your complete Fluency Pattern - a visual representation of your current state, targets, and gaps.

## Key Principles

### Fluency is Aspirational
The desired levels of fluency will be different for each capability, for each organization, and even across different parts of the same organization.

### Non-Uniform Progression
Teams don't need to be at the same level across all dimensions. Some dimensions may need to advance before others can progress.

### Incremental Steps
Each level builds on the previous one. Moving up one level at a time is the recommended approach.

### Context Matters
The right level depends on your team's goals, constraints, and business context. There's no one-size-fits-all answer.

## Timeframes

Based on typical organizational change patterns:

| Transition | Typical Timeframe |
|------------|-------------------|
| Level 1 → 2 | 3-6 months |
| Level 2 → 3 | 6-12 months |
| Level 3 → 4 | 12-18 months |
| Level 4 → 5 | 18-36 months |

These are guidelines, not rules. Your actual timeline will depend on your starting point, resources, and organizational readiness.
